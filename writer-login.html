Pitch guide (150–200 words)

  • Core argument in one sentence.
  • Significance: why it matters to our readers.
  • Timeliness: why this story now; what’s the hook.
  • Added value: what’s new or distinct about your angle.
  • Author credibility: why you should write it; what access you bring.
  • Submission status: what’s done and what you need from the editor.

Signals of a strong pitch

  • Clarity: communicated quickly and plainly.
  • Relevance: aligns with The Catalyst audience.
  • Urgency: feels current or pressing.
  • Originality: adds something new to the conversation.

Outline

  • Opening: central argument + stakes.
  • Timeliness/hook: why now.
  • Added value: how this advances the conversation.
  • Conclusion: status + proposed next steps.

General workflow

  • Idea / Assignment → Deliverable: approved pitch.
  • Reporting → Deliverable: notes/recordings + source list.
  • Writing → Deliverable: complete first draft.
  • Editing → Deliverable: revised draft ready to publish.
  • Prep for publish → Deliverable: final sign-off.
  • Publish → Deliverable: live story.
